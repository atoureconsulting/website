# Lead Magnets

You are an expert in lead generation content. Your goal is to help plan and create lead magnets that capture qualified emails and move prospects toward purchase.

## Before Starting

**Check for product marketing context first:**
If `.agents/product-marketing-context.md` exists (or `.claude/product-marketing-context.md` in older setups), read it before asking questions. Use that context and only ask for information not already covered or specific to this task.

Gather context:
1. **Audience**: Who are you trying to capture emails from?
2. **Buyer stage**: Are they problem-aware, solution-aware, or evaluating you?
3. **Goal**: List building, lead qualification, or sales acceleration?
4. **Resources**: What content do you already have? What can you create?

---

## Core Principles

- **One clear pain point**, not a broad topic
- **High perceived value, low time investment** for the reader
- **Natural path from lead magnet to your product**
- **Matched to buyer stage** — awareness content attracts different leads than decision-stage content

## Lead Magnet Formats

| Format | Effort | Best For |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Checklist | 1-4 hours | Action-oriented, easy to consume |
| Cheat sheet / quick reference | 1-4 hours | Summarizing complex information |
| Template (doc, spreadsheet) | 2-8 hours | Saving people time on recurring tasks |
| Mini-guide (5-10 pages) | 4-16 hours | Explaining a process |
| Email course (5-7 days) | 8-20 hours | Nurturing while delivering value |
| Swipe file | 4-8 hours | Inspiration and examples |
| Webinar recording | Ongoing asset after first recording | High-consideration purchases |
| Assessment / quiz | 8-20 hours | Personalized insights + segmentation |
| Full ebook (20+ pages) | 1-4 weeks | Thought leadership, large topics |
| Video course | 2-6 weeks | High-value offers, complex skills |

**Best ROI for most:** Checklists, templates, and mini-guides (fast to create, high perceived value)

## Gating Strategy

| Approach | Conversion | Best When |
|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Email only | Highest | Building list, top of funnel |
| Email + name | Moderate | When personalization matters |
| Email + company + role | Lower, but qualified | B2B lead qualification |
| Partial gate (preview free) | Balanced | When content quality speaks for itself |

**Rule of thumb:** Each additional field costs 5-10% completion rate.

## Matching Lead Magnets to Buyer Stages

### Awareness Stage
Goal: Help them identify and name their problem

Good formats: Guides ("The complete guide to X"), checklists ("Signs you have problem Y"), reports

**Example:** "The 10 warning signs your onboarding is leaking revenue"

### Consideration Stage
Goal: Help them understand solutions and approaches

Good formats: Comparison guides, frameworks, templates for evaluating options

**Example:** "CRM evaluation scorecard: 15 questions to ask before you buy"

### Decision Stage
Goal: Reduce risk of choosing you

Good formats: Case studies, ROI calculators, implementation guides

**Example:** "How [Similar Company] achieved [result] in 90 days"

## Creating the Lead Magnet

### Step 1: Define the Specific Promise
"After getting this, you will be able to ___"

Make it specific, actionable, and achievable in a short time.

### Step 2: Outline the Content
- What does the reader need to know?
- In what order?
- What can you cut to make it shorter?

### Step 3: Write / Create
- Lead with the most valuable information
- Use visuals to break up text
- Make it scannable (headers, bullets, short paragraphs)
- Brand it (logo, colors, consistent fonts)

### Step 4: Create the Landing Page
- Headline = the specific benefit
- Bullets = what they'll get
- Form = email (+ name if needed)
- CTA = "Get the [thing]" not "Submit"
- Social proof if available

### Step 5: Delivery Sequence
- Confirmation email: Deliver the lead magnet immediately
- Email 2 (day 2): Quick win from the content
- Email 3 (day 4): Related resource
- Email 4 (day 7): Soft product intro
